# 🏥 Hospital Charges: Healthcare Cost & Billing Efficiency Analysis

## 👋 Overview

This project examines healthcare billing data to uncover the drivers behind hospital charges and Medicare reimbursements. By analyzing patient discharges and Diagnosis-Related Groups (DRGs), I identified significant cost variations across states and providers, providing transparency into where billing inefficiencies and payment disparities occur within the healthcare system.

## 🚀 Key Features & Analysis

  * **State-Level Benchmarking:** Performed a geographic analysis to identify states and regions with the highest charge-to-payment ratios.
  * **DRG Cost Profiling:** Identified which medical diagnoses (DRGs) drive the highest costs and evaluated their reimbursement efficiency.
  * **Charge vs. Payment Gap:** Quantified the disparity between what hospitals bill ("Covered Charges") and the actual "Total Payments" received.
  * **Volume-Efficiency Correlation:** Investigated whether higher patient discharge volumes lead to better payment efficiency or lower unit costs.
  * **Provider Outlier Detection:** Isolated specific hospitals with billing patterns that deviate significantly from state and national averages.

## 🛠️ Excel Toolkit Used

  * **High-Performance Modeling:** Optimized the analysis of large-scale healthcare records using **Excel Binary Workbook (.xlsb)** format to maintain calculation speed.
  * **Data Engineering:** Cleaned hospital-DRG records, handled missing values, and standardized state abbreviations for accurate mapping.
  * **Financial Auditing:** Derived complex metrics like "Payment Gap" and "Cost Intensity" using advanced Excel formulas.
  * **Pivot Analysis:** Built multi-dimensional Pivot Tables to aggregate performance by Diagnosis, Provider, and Location.
  * **Statistical Profiling:** Used standard deviation and benchmarking indicators to flag pricing outliers and billing anomalies.

## ⚙️ How It Works

1.  **Phase 1: Performance Setup** – Converted raw data to `.xlsb` to efficiently handle the large volume of healthcare records and provider details.
2.  **Phase 2: Descriptive Analysis** – Profiled the national average for Medicare reimbursements vs. billed charges to establish a baseline.
3.  **Phase 3: Efficiency Evaluation** – Categorized hospitals into "Cost Intensity" groups to identify the most and least efficient providers.
4.  **Phase 4: Policy Insights** – Synthesized findings into a professional report aimed at improving transparency and cost-control in healthcare billing.

## 📈 Key Business Insights

  * **The Payment Disparity:** Analysis revealed that Medicare payments often cover only a small fraction of billed charges, highlighting a critical lack of pricing transparency.
  * **Diagnosis Drivers:** Certain complex DRGs were identified as "cost-escalators," suggesting where hospitals should focus internal cost-control efforts.
  * **The Volume Paradox:** Data proved that higher discharge volumes do not always guarantee better payment efficiency, often indicating hidden operational bottlenecks.
  * **State Variance:** Significant variances in charges for the identical procedure were found across state lines, indicating a need for regional pricing benchmarks.
